What is Weight Lifting?

Weight Lifting sounds pretty straightforward...picking up weight, but it can be suprisingly complex. Weight lifting entails strength training through a variety of forms of resistance such as free weights, bands or machines to build muscle and improve physical wellness.

Types of weight lifting?

  • Power Lifting: Involves lifting the most weight possible.
  • Bodybuilding: Involves lifting weight to maximize hypertrophy (growth) focusing on aesthetics and muscle definition.
  • Olympic Lifting: Involves two primary movements: the snatch and the clean & jerk. This type focuses on explosive power and technique.

Best Times to Workout

What time should you workout? The time you workout impacts so much more than you think. If you go to the gym early in the morning and you are not a morning person more than likely you are setting yourself up for failure. However also going to the gym at common times such as 5pm or 6pm you are going to be waiting a long time to do exercises that you want to do. The gym will be packed and you'll lose your muscle pump because of the amount of time you are waiting to use a machine or weight. You CAN workout at night such as 8pm and onward but if you take caffeine such as preworkout you will be bouncing off the walls trying to fall asleep. Caffeine takes a long time to clear from your system you have to be mindful of the amount and time you consume it at.

At Home

Working out at home has numerous benefits varying from being in the privacy of your own home. To not having to deal with some of the annoying social aspects such as loud grunting or weights falling to the ground. Another benefit that working out from home has is that your home is always open and you can workout whenever, its not like the gym where you have a time restriction. In the When section of my page I spoke about the time you go to the gym and how much it matters because of how crowded the gym can get at times. Well you never have to experience that when you are working out from home. Going to the gym or other establishments costs money working out at home is 100% free with the exception of building a home gym and buying free weights you can get a perfectly good workout with just your body! Working out from home is great however it does have some pitfalls such as, not being able to do certain exercises because of the lack of equipment. For example the amount of back exercises or leg exercises you can do is limited when you are working out at home compared to the gym or another place with equipment. Another downside of working out at home is the lack of social interaction, to some this may seem like a benefit but depending on your lifestyle if you are constantly alone being in a social space will boost your social life! The final con of working out at home are the distractions you are surrounded by them. At any time you can walk up to your bed and nap completely ignoring your workout.

The Gym

Working out at the gym comes with its own set of advantages, primarily the wide variety of equipment available to target every muscle group. Whether it's free weights, machines, or cardio equipment, the gym provides the tools to achieve a well-rounded workout. Another significant pro is the professional atmosphere—the gym is a dedicated space for fitness, which can help mentally separate workout time from personal time. Additionally, the gym often offers classes and group workouts that provide structure, guidance, and motivation, something you don't get when working out at home. Having access to personal trainers also adds an element of expertise that can improve form and help you achieve your goals faster. However, there are some drawbacks to working out in the gym as well. One of the most common is the time commitment—getting to and from the gym can take time out of your day, not to mention gym hours and membership fees. It can also get crowded, especially during peak hours, which may limit access to equipment or make the experience less enjoyable. Some gyms may not be as clean as you'd like, or you might find it uncomfortable sharing equipment with others. Another downside is the pressure of gym culture; for some, the social aspect of the gym can be intimidating, making it harder to focus or feel confident during workouts. Lastly, depending on your goals, the gym might not always provide the quiet, personal environment some people need to fully concentrate on their workout.

Pro's Summarized List

  • Wide Variety Of Equipment
  • Professional Atmosphere
  • Access To Personal Trainers

Con's Summarized List

  • Commuting
  • Crowded
  • Gym culture and social pressure can make some feel uncomfortable or distracted

Why?

  • Confidence: Going to the gym will boost your confidence ten fold. You will look better, feel better, and most of all be able to push yourself to go further even when things are hard.
  • Health: Your general health and wellness will increase because of this new physical activity that you will be doing.
  • Discipline and Routine: Incorporating weight lifting into your routine fosters discipline, structure, and healthy habits that extend into other areas of life.
  • Longevity: Studies show that people who regularly lift weights may increase their lifespan due to improved overall health and resilience.
